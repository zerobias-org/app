Strategic Insights

✓ The 4-level hierarchy works
Engagement → Activity → Task → SubTask naturally maps to real procurement documents. Exhibit F proves it: 5 categories = 5 tasks, ~45 individual requirements = 45 subtasks. No forcing needed.
⚠ Scale reality check
A single exhibit produces 45 subtasks. A full government RFP has 5–10 exhibits → 200–400+ subtasks. UI must handle this with filtering, search, domain rollups, and collapse/expand.
★ Document-to-task decomposition is the killer feature
An AI agent that parses procurement exhibits and auto-generates the task/subtask tree would be a massive differentiator vs. Upwork/Fiverr. No competitor does this. This is the agentic opportunity Brian envisions.
◆ Typed tasks are non-negotiable
Without Security/Compliance/Legal/Functional typing, you can't build domain-specific rollups or the Transparency Center. This is the foundation for everything — blocked by ZB-3 (Custom Task Activities). Flag to Kevin.
♦ Standards references add massive value
Exhibit F references NIST SP800-53, OWASP ASVS, FIPS-140, HIPAA, PCI DSS. Linking subtasks to specific standard controls enables automated compliance mapping — a premium enterprise feature.
◎ This is ONLY the demand side
Everything here is what the buyer defines. The supply side mirrors this structure with evidence, attestation, and readiness per subtask. The transparency center shows where they meet.
