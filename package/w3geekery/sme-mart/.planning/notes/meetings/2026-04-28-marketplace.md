## Topics Discussed

### 1. Vendor / Product Two-Sided Partition (architecture spike)
Brian opened with a major construct: every vendor and product needs a **two-sided partition** — a **community-built** side and a **vendor-claimed (producer)** side. The community may catalog/document/integrate a vendor's product before the vendor ever shows up on the platform; when the vendor does claim, they take ownership of *their* partition only. They can never delete or manipulate the community partition. The two sides may disagree, and that disagreement is intentional — the platform's job is to surface "machine-verifiable truth" via API operations and queries (e.g., does feature X actually exist?).

Validation for vendor-claimed side: domain ownership, email at domain, tax ID, Dun & Bradstreet, etc. Smaller vendors will be motivated to claim and integrate; larger vendors ("the big boys") may ignore the platform — community must be able to route around them.

### 2. SME Mart Pricing Model
Single-digit (~3%) "toll booth" cut on all marketplace transactions — AWS / Apple App Store / Shopify model. Sellers publish their pricing/charge model into SME Mart; buyers transact through the marketplace. **Selling outside the marketplace = banned + large fines** for participants. SME Mart is "nothing but a toll booth" for now.

Future possibility: tiered listing fees (e.g., ~$100/mo to post an app), possibly favoring smaller businesses. Not building this yet — "obvious when we get there."

### 3. Per-App Terms of Service / EULA / MSA Architecture
Two layers, mirroring AWS:
- **Engagement-level MSA** — required to have a ZB account at all (analog: AWS account-level ToS).
- **Per-app/product ToS + consumption model + price model** — every ZB platform offering (Value Manager, Governance, SME Mart itself, etc.) is its own packaged product with its own ToS and chargeback rules.

ZeroBias-as-platform will end up with ~30 declarative product packages, just like AWS does.

When the pilot graduates, additional details (banking, fuller MSA) are collected and the **same project** transitions from "pilot" type to "production" type — project entity is preserved.

## Key Decisions

1. **Two-sided product partition is canonical** — community side and producer side coexist; producer can never delete community side; community can refute producer claims with machine-verifiable proof.
2. **Marketplace monetization = 3% transactional toll only** for now; tiered listing fees deferred.
3. **Per-app ToS is the model** — every ZB app gets its own ToS, consumption model, and chargeback rules; SME Mart is one such app.
4. **Pilot vs Production project type** — same project entity, type flips when engagement graduates from pilot/trial to permanent.
5. **Brian-questions Q&A flow** — Clark will keep these as requirement-style tasks in the W3Geekery↔ZB project notes; Brian (eventually Claude-enabled) responds to tasks or to the underlying APIs.
