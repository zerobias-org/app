## Bid Process Workflow (4 Phases)

### Phase 1: RFP Distribution & Kickoff
1. Buyer issues RFP to invited vendors only
2. Mandatory kick-off conference (Teams video) — attendance required for compliance
3. Random draw for individual vendor conference slots
4. Vendors review requirements

### Phase 2: Vendor Response
1. Submit **Appendix B** (Intent to Propose) OR **Appendix C** (Intent to Withdraw) — failure = non-compliant
2. Submit corporate stability + financial statements within 10 business days
3. Submit **Appendix D** (Contact Information) — primary, executive, technical, 6 additional contacts
4. Submit **Exhibit A** (Non-Discrimination Affidavit) — notarized
5. Submit written questions via email to Program Manager only
6. Attend dedicated 2-hour vendor conference (agenda includes Q&A, technical discussion, proposed relationship)
7. Submit complete bid response following RFP section numbering

### Phase 3: Evaluation & Selection
1. Evaluation committee reviews responses (internal + external members)
2. Single finalist selected (no multi-finalist process)
3. Legal contracting begins immediately upon selection

### Phase 4: Contract Execution
1. 0-80-20 payment structure (0% down, 80% at testing complete, 20% after 30-day acceptance)
2. Support/maintenance starts after acceptance period
3. Training must complete before 30-day acceptance begins
4. Proposal valid for 180 days

## Vendor Qualification Requirements

Beyond technical response, vendors must provide:

1. **Corporate stability statement** — financial health narrative
2. **Financial statements** — public: Annual Report + SEC filings; private: audited financials
3. **Named contacts** — primary, executive, technical, + up to 6 additional
4. **Legal resources** — identify contract negotiation team members
5. **Staff resumes** — project manager and technical engineers
6. **Subcontractor disclosure** — all third parties, subject to buyer approval
7. **Bid/performance bond willingness** — must confirm ability to provide if requested
8. **Non-discrimination affidavit** — notarized attestation
9. **Reference customers** — implied by evaluation process
10. **Insurance/liability** — implied by contracting requirements

## Key Takeaway

This RFP represents a **mature, enterprise procurement process** with a professional consultant managing the workflow. It's significantly more structured than the CDPH RFP — more forms, stricter communication rules, complex pricing, and formal vendor qualification steps. SME Mart needs to support both the "post and browse" marketplace model AND this kind of invitation-only, heavily mediated procurement process.
