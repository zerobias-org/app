Section 1 – Introduction and Overview 
This Request for Proposal (hereafter called the RFP) is issued by the Department of General Services, Procurement Division (DGS-PD) on behalf of the California Department of Public Health (CDPH). The RFP solicits proposals from firms qualified to establish a contract to provide a data subscription for a pre-existing, Contractor-hosted Software as a Service (SaaS) solution that displays real‑time staffed bed availability by bed type on a secure dashboard accessible to participating hospitals and local and state government officials. The solution is referred to as the Hospital Bed and Emergency Medical Services Data System (HBEDS).

Scope of the Request for Proposal (RFP) 
The Scope of the RFP includes an HBEDS SaaS solution and services as defined in Exhibit A, Scope of Work (SOW).  

The State intends that the selected Bidder will work in cooperation with the State to furnish quality software access and services at the lowest price available in a timely and efficient manner. The selected Bidder will be the primary point of contact for all contract requirements.

Award will be made on an All or None basis to the Bidder who scores the highest number of points as described in Section 6, Evaluation.   Bidders must propose all line items to be evaluated for award. The State reserves the right to eliminate any specific line item from the final award.

The contract term resulting from the RFP will be three (3) years.  The contract will also contain options to extend for two (2) additional one-year periods, or portion thereof. If a mutual agreement cannot be reached, the contract will expire at the end of the current contract term.  All terms and conditions will remain the same for the entire contract period, including any extension periods.

Any contract executed as a result of the RFP may be amended by mutual consent of the State and the Contractor.

Authority of the RFP and Bidder Advisement 
The RFP is being conducted under the policies developed by the Department of General Services, Procurement Division (DGS-PD) as provided under PCC sections 12102, et seq and 12125, et seq. The RFP describes the software and services that must be included in the proposal, the format the proposal must follow, and how to properly submit a complete proposal. The RFP also addresses all requirements that Bidders must meet to be eligible for consideration as well as Bidders' responsibilities.
 
If a Bidder expects to be afforded the benefits of the steps included in this RFP (i.e., drafts, questions regarding requirements, protest of requirements, debriefing), the Bidder must take responsibility to:

Carefully read the entire RFP.
Ask appropriate questions in a timely manner if clarification is necessary.
Submit all required responses, complete to the best of the Bidder’s ability, by the required dates and times.
Make sure that all procedures and requirements of the RFP are accurately followed and appropriately addressed. 

Availability 
Bidders may propose software and services that exceed the RFP requirements, however, software and services meeting the mandatory requirements must be available throughout the duration of the contract term.

Section 2 – Rules Governing the Procurement Process
Section 2 describes the proposal steps and includes the Bidder’s and the State’s rights and responsibilities in the procurement process. Specific guidelines for the submission of the RFP response are found in Section 5, Proposal Format.

Identification and Classification of RFP Requirements

Requirements
The RFP identifies the requirements Bidders must adhere to for submitting compliant proposals. The use of "shall," "must," or "will" (except to indicate simple futurity) in the RFP indicates a requirement which is mandatory. A deviation from a mandatory requirement is material if the deficient response is not in substantial accord with the RFP requirements, provides an advantage to one Bidder over other Bidders, or has a potentially significant effect on the delivery, quantity, or quality of items proposed, amount paid to the supplier, or on the cost to the State.  Material deviations cannot be waived. A deviation, if not material, may be waived by the State.  

Desirable Items
The words "should" or "may" in the RFP indicate desirable attributes or conditions but are non-mandatory in nature.  Deviation from, or omission of, such a desirable feature, will not in itself cause rejection of the proposal.

Mandatory Requirements
All items within the RFP labeled mandatory (M) are non-negotiable. A Pass/Fail evaluation will be utilized for all mandatory requirements. By signing the Agreement Cover Letter referenced in Section 2.3.4.5, Signature of Proposal and submission of a proposal response, the Bidder agrees to all mandatory RFP requirements. 

Non-Mandatory Requirements
The RFP may include both preference programs and product/contract performance items identified as non-mandatory (NM) requirements. Bidders are not required to comply with these requirements to be compliant with the RFP. However, if a Bidder applies for a preference, the Bidder must meet the applicable preference requirements and provide the required preference documentation as outlined in the relevant section. Bidders who offer the non-mandatory product/contract performance items in their proposal are required, if awarded, to provide those items for the duration of the resultant contract. 

Scored Requirements
The RFP may include both mandatory scored (MS) and non-mandatory scored (NMS) requirements. Mandatory scored requirements are non-negotiable. Mandatory and non-mandatory scored items will achieve points as identified Section 6, Evaluation, if the requirement is met. 


Narrative Responses

Preference Calculation

Small Business Preference Calculation
If the Small Business (SB) preference is to be applied to a California-certified Small Business’ Total Score, the following formula will be utilized:

SB Preference Points = (Highest Non-Small Business Total Score) multiplied by (5 percent) 
SB Preference Points will be added to the total score of responsive proposals eligible to receive the preference.

Example: 
Highest non-small business’ Total Score: 1000 points 
SB Preference: 1000 points multiplied by 0.05 = 50 points 
The 50 points would be added to the total score for all eligible Bidders 

DVBE Incentive Calculation
If a DVBE Incentive is to be applied to a qualified Bidder’s total score, the Incentive calculation will be accordance with the California DVBE Bid Incentive Instructions.

Example for Bidder Claiming 5 percent: 
Highest Bidder’s Total Score: 1000 points 
DVBE Incentive: 1000 points multiplied by 0.05 = 50 points 
The 50 points would be added to the total score for all eligible Bidders claiming 5 percent participation.

Note, if the Bidder with the highest point total is a small business the DVBE incentive only applies to a small business claiming the incentive.
