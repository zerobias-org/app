EXHIBIT A - STATEMENT OF WORK (SOW)

PROJECT BACKGROUND 
The California Department of Public Health, Center for Preparedness and Response (hereafter referred to as “CDPH” or “the State”), coordinates overall emergency planning and preparedness efforts. CDPH plans and executes activities to prepare Californians for public health emergencies, oversee statewide public health disaster planning, and maintains and manages the Medical and Health Coordination Center (MHCC).

DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED SYSTEM
The Contractor shall provide a data subscription, contractor-hosted, pre-existing Software as a Service (SaaS) solution with hospital onboarding services to support CDPH compliance with Assembly Bill 177 (AB 177), Chapter 999, Section 3, for the development and administration of healthcare bed capacity. The Hospital Bed and Emergency Medical Services Data System (HBEDS) shall display real-time staffed bed availability by bed type on a secure dashboard accessible to participating hospitals and local and state government officials. HBEDS shall be automated through hospital Electronic Medical Records/Electronic Health Records (EMR/EHR) systems, replacing manual bed-polling processes. The platform shall include functionality to collect data from specified healthcare entities, including bed census and availability by level of care, unit, and hospital, and shall report this data to support statewide situational awareness and emergency response coordination.

SAAS SYSTEM SUBSCRIPTION/LICENSE

SOLUTION & SERVICE REQUIREMENTS
The Contractor shall provide a data subscription, contractor-hosted, pre-existing Software as a Service (SaaS) solution that meets the State’s requirements for secure access, and enables the State to obtain healthcare hospital bed availability data in real time, with the following capabilities and features:

System Integration, Data Collection, and Security

Capable of integration and support with healthcare hospital HER/EMR systems to receive data on staffed bed availability by bed type used by healthcare hospitals in California.

Collect data elements from hospitals on a continuous, 24/7 basis, in 15‑minute increments, as required by the Center for Disease Control and Prevention National Healthcare Safety Network (CDC/NHSN). The instructions can be found in Exhibit A1, HBEDS CDC-NHSN Instruction Book. 

A full list of required data elements is provided in Exhibit A2, HBEDS CDC-NHSN Required Data Elements. The Contractor shall ensure the software incorporates and maintains all data elements listed in the Exhibit A2, HBEDS CDC-NHSN Required Data Elements. These are the data elements the Contractor is required to submit to the CDC/NHSN and are subject to change. If CDC/NHSN reporting requirements change, the Contractor shall comply with all applicable HBEDS requirements, notify the CDPH Contract Administrator and Project Manager, and adhere to all transmission and import guidelines. Additional information is available in Hospital Bed Capacity Project | NHSN |CDC. 

These requirements include, but are not limited to, the following:

Hospital inpatient and ICU bed capacity and occupancy data, overall and by bed type (i.e., pediatric and adult).

Numbers of hospitalized patients with lab-confirmed COVID-19, Influenza, and RSV, by age group.
Numbers of new hospital admissions of patients with lab-confirmed COVID-19, Influenza, and RSV, by age group.
  
Data should be retained by the Contractor for historical visualization purposes on the dashboard as outlined in Section B, Dashboard.

Single Sign On (SSO) is required by using Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) 2.0 or OpenID Connect (OIDC)/Open Authorization 2 (OAuth2) protocols.

Multi Factor Authentication (MFA) is required by all; (a) privileged access, (b) user access to sensitive or confidential data, and (c) accounts representing official communications from state departments.

Automate bed reporting on behalf of healthcare hospitals. 

Support data transmission via Secure File Transfer Protocol (SFTP), Application Programming Interface (API), or Fast Health Interoperability Resources (FHIR), depending on each hospital’s capabilities.

Dashboard 

The dashboard shall include, as needed but not limited to, the following elements: 

Filtering by hospital, bed type, and region. 

Enable users to download historical data collected after enrollment. 

Depict weekly, monthly, annual, and specified date filters overtime for participating hospitals.

Real Time Bed Availability
Number of staffed beds by type (ICU, medical/surgical, pediatric, psychiatric, etc.).
Occupancy rates and available capacity across hospitals.

Hospital Integration Status
Connectivity with EHR/EMR systems.
Success/failure logs of data feeds.

Operational Insights
Trends in bed usage over time.
Surge capacity indicators for emergency activation.
Geographic distribution of available beds across California.

CDC/NHSN Reporting Compliance
Timeliness of submissions and audit logs.
Alerts for missed or delayed updates.
Transmission

Transmit and submit required bed capacity data on behalf of enrolled hospitals to CDC/NHSN at least twice daily as described on Exhibit A1, CDC/NHSN Instruction Book.

Transmit data to CDPH via API every 15 minutes to support internal analysis and reporting.
